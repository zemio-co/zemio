## 2. Background and Roles

The controller uses the Zemio service to manage expense-report workflows within its organization.

To the extent Zemio processes personal data on behalf of the controller in connection with customer-managed report data, expense data, attachment data, banking data, invitation workflows, and related organization-specific service content, Zemio acts as processor within the meaning of Article 28 GDPR.

Processing activities carried out by Zemio as an independent controller, including platform security, billing, provider administration, and general service management, are not governed by this Data Processing Agreement except to the extent they overlap with processor obligations under applicable law.

## 3. Subject Matter and Duration

The subject matter of the processing is the provision of the Zemio software service for the controller.

The duration of the processing corresponds to the term of the main service contract plus any subsequent period during which Zemio stores or deletes personal data in accordance with the contract, documented customer instructions, legal obligations, or backup retention windows.

## 4. Nature and Purpose of Processing

Processing under this Data Processing Agreement may include:

- hosting customer organization data;
- storing and displaying expense reports and related metadata;
- storing and making available attachments such as receipts, invoices, images, and PDFs;
- storing encrypted banking details where entered by users;
- authenticating users and assigning users to organizations;
- sending operational emails triggered by customer use of the service;
- supporting role-based access, review workflows, exports, backups, and incident handling;
- deleting or returning data on request or after contract termination.

## 5. Categories of Data Subjects

Data subjects may include:

- users of the controller's organization;
- organization administrators and reviewers;
- invited users;
- customer representatives and billing contacts;
- individuals identified in uploaded expense documentation or attachment content, where applicable.

## 6. Categories of Personal Data

Depending on the controller's use of the service, processed personal data may include:

- name, email address, profile information, user role, and organization membership;
- Microsoft tenant-related identity data;
- authentication and session metadata, including IP address and user agent;
- invitation data;
- report and expense data;
- attachment files and metadata;
- encrypted banking details, including IBAN and account holder name;
- notification preferences and operational communication data.

Special categories of personal data under Article 9 GDPR are not intentionally required for the service and should not be uploaded unless the controller has a valid legal basis and has expressly determined that such use is necessary.

## 7. Documented Instructions

Zemio shall process personal data only on documented instructions from the controller, unless Zemio is required to do otherwise by Union or Member State law to which Zemio is subject.

The following shall be deemed documented instructions:

- the main service contract and agreed service configuration;
- these contractual documents;
- use of the service by authorized users within its intended functionality;
- individual written instructions from the controller's authorized contacts.

If Zemio believes that an instruction infringes applicable data protection law, Zemio shall inform the controller without undue delay unless prohibited by law.

## 8. Confidentiality

Zemio shall ensure that persons authorized to process personal data have committed themselves to confidentiality or are under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality.

## 9. Security of Processing

Zemio shall implement appropriate technical and organizational measures in accordance with Article 32 GDPR, taking into account the state of the art, implementation costs, the nature, scope, context, and purposes of processing, and the risks to the rights and freedoms of natural persons.

The measures currently applied are described in the TOMs annex to this Data Processing Agreement.

## 10. Assistance to the Controller

Taking into account the nature of the processing and the information available to Zemio, Zemio shall assist the controller by appropriate technical and organizational measures, insofar as possible, for:

- responding to requests for exercising data subject rights;
- ensuring compliance with Articles 32 to 36 GDPR;
- handling personal data breaches;
- carrying out data protection impact assessments where required.

Where a data subject submits a request directly to Zemio concerning data processed on behalf of the controller, Zemio may forward the request to the controller unless legally prohibited.

## 11. Personal Data Breach Notification

If Zemio becomes aware of a personal data breach affecting personal data processed on behalf of the controller, Zemio shall notify the controller without undue delay and provide available information reasonably necessary for the controller to assess and address the breach.

## 12. Subprocessors

The controller grants Zemio a general authorization to engage subprocessors.

The subprocessors currently used for the service are listed in the subprocessor list accompanying this agreement.

Zemio shall:

- impose data protection obligations on subprocessors that are no less protective than those set out in this agreement to the extent applicable to the subprocessor's role;
- remain responsible for the performance of its subprocessors to the extent required by Article 28 GDPR.

Zemio shall inform the controller of material intended changes concerning the addition or replacement of subprocessors in an appropriate manner, allowing the controller to raise reasonable data protection objections.

## 13. International Transfers

Where Zemio or a subprocessor transfers personal data to a third country, Zemio shall ensure that the transfer is based on a lawful transfer mechanism under Chapter V GDPR, such as an adequacy decision or the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses.

## 14. Audit and Information Rights

Zemio shall make available to the controller information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with Article 28 GDPR and this agreement.

Audits by the controller or an auditor mandated by the controller shall be permitted to the extent required by law and subject to:

- reasonable prior notice;
- appropriate confidentiality safeguards;
- protection of trade secrets and security measures;
- avoidance of disproportionate disruption;
- reimbursement of reasonable costs where the audit exceeds ordinary documentation review and no material breach by Zemio is established.

Zemio may satisfy audit obligations by providing suitable documentation, certifications, summaries, or questionnaire responses where appropriate.

## 15. Deletion and Return of Data

Upon termination of the main contract, Zemio shall, at the choice of the controller where technically feasible and timely requested:

- return personal data in a reasonably available format; or
- delete personal data,

unless Union or Member State law requires storage of the personal data.

Backup copies and residual technical copies may persist temporarily for a limited retention period where this is inherent in secure backup procedures, provided continued protection measures apply.

## 16. Liability

Liability under this Data Processing Agreement follows the liability allocation of the main contract, subject to mandatory data protection law.

## 17. Governing Law

This Data Processing Agreement is governed by German law, subject to mandatory data protection law.
